The Hong Kong Protests - And why it matters for Canadians

When China proposed an extradition bill that would allow China to extradite anyone who has supposedly commited a crime in China, Hong Kong residents took to the streets in protest of it. These were peaceful walkouts from people who understood the greater implications of this bill, and how life might look for them if it passed - how life already looks in China. For 3 months, the HK government ambled along, watching as the situation escalated between the police force and the protestors. The police became more and more violent, even going so far as to restrain first responders trying to help the protestors, yet not a single officer was arrested. Now, the bill has been withdrawn, but the protestors are looking for justice in their police force. Their demands:

  • Withdrawal of classification of peaceful protests as "Rioting" - Rioting carries a 10 year sentence in Hong Kong
  • Unconditional release of arrested protestors and dropping of charges
  • An independent inquiry into police behaviour
  • Genuine democracy - Hong Kong's system is not a true democracy
  • Complete withdrawal of the extradition bill - Completed!
